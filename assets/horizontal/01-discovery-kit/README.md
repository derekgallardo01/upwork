# 01 · Discovery & scoping kit

> Turn a vague client brief into a scoped, fixed-price-able statement of work in
> one call — so projects start with aligned expectations and you never scope-creep
> for free.

**Covers (feed clusters):** every engagement. The Telecom job literally made
"Discovery & Requirements" its Phase 1; most consult/agent/automation posts ask
for an approach before they hire.

## What's inside

- [`discovery-questionnaire.md`](discovery-questionnaire.md) — the questions to ask
  on (or before) the first call: goals, current setup, data, constraints, success
  criteria, access.
- [`sow-template.md`](sow-template.md) — a fill-in statement-of-work: scope,
  out-of-scope, deliverables, milestones, assumptions, change process, price.
- [`OFFER.md`](OFFER.md) — paste-ready Project Catalog listing.

## How to use

1. Send the questionnaire ahead of the discovery call (or work through it live).
2. Fill the SOW from the answers; the **Out of scope** and **Assumptions**
   sections are what protect your margin — never skip them.
3. Get a thumbs-up on the SOW *before* building. For fixed-price jobs, the SOW is
   your scope boundary; for hourly, it's your shared plan.

This single habit — scoping in writing first — is the cheapest way to avoid the
two things that wreck freelance jobs: unpaid scope creep and mismatched
expectations.
