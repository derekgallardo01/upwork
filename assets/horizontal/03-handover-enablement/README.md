# 03 · Handover & enablement pack

> Every project ends with documentation a non-technical owner can actually use —
> a handover guide, an edit-it-later runbook, and a short recorded walkthrough — so
> clients feel safe, leave 5-star reviews, and come back.

**Covers (feed clusters):** the Asana job *required* a "short handover guide
(screenshots + steps)"; Amazon Connect wanted "document and video a step-by-step
solution"; Copilot&Claude wanted "step-by-step instructions"; Telecom wanted
"knowledge transfer." Handover is the single most-requested deliverable in the feed.

## What's inside

- [`handover-guide-template.md`](handover-guide-template.md) — what was built, how
  it works, how to monitor it, who to call.
- [`runbook-template.md`](runbook-template.md) — the "edit it later" runbook: common
  changes, troubleshooting, recovery.
- [`loom-script.md`](loom-script.md) — a 3–5 minute walkthrough script (most
  freelancers never record one — it's a cheap differentiator and a review magnet).
- [`OFFER.md`](OFFER.md) — paste-ready Project Catalog listing.

## How to use

1. Fill the handover guide and runbook as you build (screenshots as you go — don't
   leave it to the end).
2. Record the Loom from the script; drop the link in the handover guide.
3. Deliver all three at project close and walk the client through them live.

Good handover is why clients write "would hire again" — it converts a one-off gig
into reviews, repeat work, and referrals.
